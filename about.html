About Luke

Local help from someone who likes making strange new technologies and ideas feel lighter and clearer.

Strange but True is a local mix of technical support, creative media, music, writing and practical planning.

Market presence

A public table, not a shopfront.

The market stall gives people a simple way to meet the idea in person: ask a tech question, browse creative samples, talk through a local project, or make a first connection before booking proper help.

Why this exists

A lot of useful technology gets wrapped in stress, jargon or pressure. Strange but True is built around a simpler invitation: bring the confusing thing, the local idea, the half-formed project or the creative spark, and make it easier to work with.

The work is mobile, market-based and by arrangement across North Stradbroke Island / Minjerribah.

The creative side connects with Luke's existing music and profile projects, including I See Infinity and Luke Catalyst. Strange but True keeps that imagination close, while staying focused on practical local help.
