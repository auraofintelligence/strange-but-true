A local thread, not a straight line.

My connection to North Stradbroke Island / Minjerribah runs through family, time and return. My grandmother, Constance Millar, was active in island community life and volunteering for around 50 years before she passed away in late February 2025.

I have regularly visited and also resided here on and off for years between travel, work, study, renovations, family responsibilities and periods away. After returning home from India in 2024, the island became the clearest place to gather the useful parts of my life into one practical local service.

The short version

I have taken a winding path, but the offer is simple: bring the confusing device, form, AI tool, website, grant idea, event plan or creative problem, and I will help make it clearer.

Strange but True

Strange enough to intrigue, true enough to trust.

I usually cut my own path. That has taken me through many jobs, travel, music festivals, websites, writing, AI experiments, creative projects and unfinished formal study where the pace or direction did not fit. The pattern underneath is not chaos for its own sake. It is curiosity, independence and a long-running interest in how technology, creativity and community systems can help people instead of overwhelming them.

My bigger life work points toward what I call Joyful Responsible Abundance: a future where powerful tools are used carefully, playfully and for the benefit of ordinary people, local communities and the living world. That idea can get very large, very quickly, so this website keeps the first step simple.

Two small lines from older poems still sit underneath the work: "seek deeper meaning" and "Make your mark." They belong here as quiet signposts, not as homework for anyone who just needs practical help.

First step

You do not need to understand the whole galaxy of ideas to ask for help. Start with the practical thing in front of you. We can make that easier first.

Why Strange but True?

A lot of useful technology gets wrapped in stress, jargon or pressure. Strange but True is built around a simpler invitation: bring the confusing thing, the local idea, the half-formed project or the creative spark, and I'll help make it easier to work with.

I have had computers around me for almost my whole life, from Pong and Atari through the slow arrival of arcade games, the internet, websites, modern games, digital music, AI tools and the current wave of strange new systems. My love of science fiction probably helped. So did years of learning even in adversity, leaving stagnant rooms, trying different work, learning from the perspectives of others and following the thread until it became useful.

You can meet me at my market stall for a free chat. The work is mobile, so I'll come to you by arrangement, anywhere across North Stradbroke Island / Minjerribah.

The creative side connects with my existing music and profile projects, including I See Infinity and Luke Catalyst. Strange but True keeps that imagination close, while staying focused on practical local help.
