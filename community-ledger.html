When the honour board begins

Public support comes after a project makes sense.

The Community Honour Board is not meant to launch as an empty gimmick. It begins when someone sees a local project, understands why it matters, and wants the public to know they support work on it.

That support may sit beside paid services such as grant writing, project mapping, AI agent flow design, stakeholder notes, web content, prototype work or another practical next step. It also helps show which projects have real momentum, which helps guide what gets prioritised next.

Public support records

Public records are optional, and only for visible supporters.

No public profile, wallet or crypto setup is required to browse the worlds, ask for help, hire a service, or bring a community idea. Those options only matter if someone chooses to make their support visible.

A public profile can connect a supporter, the project being supported, the service paid for, the amount paid, and any public wallet address they choose to use. Those records can feed a Community Honour Board dashboard.

Private context

Some useful planning stays private.

The Profile Builder creates a plain public file only when someone chooses to appear beside a project. The Aura Builder is different: it is private planning context for goals, limits, devices, AI preferences, project notes and future tools.

You only write what you are comfortable sharing, and private context does not need to become a public record.
