The first rule

Public work uses the public rail. Private work can stay private.

If someone hires Strange but True to support an existing private idea, private business, internal workflow or personal project, they can pay in fiat through the normal invoice path. If they hire Luke to build public-use community systems, civic software, grant infrastructure, AI pipelines or shared planning tools, they pay through the public crypto ledger so the support, wallet, profile and build trail can be seen.

Choose the path

Pay the quest fee

A wallet lets the player pay the agreed public-build fee on the public ledger.

This is not an invitation to invest. The player only needs enough crypto to cover the agreed fee for the quest they are paying Luke to play in the public build, using the equivalent crypto value of the fiat quote at the time of payment.

1. Confirm the fiat fee

  1. Luke quotes the public-build quest in AUD first.
  2. The payer chooses the public rail for that quest: SOL/iCi path or XRP path.
  3. The crypto amount is calculated from the agreed fiat fee.
  4. The payer does not need to buy more than the fee and normal transfer costs.

2. Buy only enough to pay

  1. Use an exchange or wallet service available in Australia to buy the needed amount.
  2. This is a payment step, not investment advice.
  3. If the payer later learns more and chooses to invest, that is separate from this game.
  4. Keep the receipt, AUD value, date and transaction reference.

3. Create the public trail

  1. The wallet becomes the payer's public game handle for that quest.
  2. SOL support can be used by Luke to buy iCi as the story signal.
  3. XRP support remains visible as the direct public transaction trail.
  4. The board links payment, wallet, profile, quest and verified output.

Coming next: setup pages that teach a new player how to buy only enough SOL or XRP to pay a quoted public-build fee, send it safely, and link the payment to a Community Ledger profile.

The purpose is reputation building in the reciprocity economy: a visible contribution trail, not speculation. Referral links may be added later only with clear disclosure and without changing the payment-first safety advice.
